# slop cannons

### rethinking organizations for the age of ai

---

> The modern engineering organization is a government contract wearing a hoodie. The people who control the resources are evaluated by how many resources they control. Directors are promoted for growing teams. Not for shrinking them.

> Calibration meetings where a person's career trajectory is negotiated by people who have never read their code. Platform initiatives with eighteen-month timelines and forty-person teams and success criteria so vague that failure is definitionally impossible because nobody defined success precisely enough to be falsified.

> The career ladder persists not because it develops people but because it is the mechanism through which the organization sees itself, and the people who run the organization are the people the ladder elevated. The structure is self-preserving. It will not reform itself.

> We made the machines elastic and the humans brittle. We scaled everything except ourselves.

> The reckoning is not coming from a book. It is coming from a generation of engineers who wield honesty the way the old guard wielded politics. They do not care about your leveling rubric. They care about whether the thing they built works and whether someone uses it.

> Think of each pair as a container. The container is elastic. It might be deployed to finance one week and sales the next. It might be composed with other containers to solve a large, high-value problem. The latent capacity of these pairs is never wasted because the pairs are never permanently assigned. Loosely coupled. High impact. Stateless in their organizational attachment. Deeply stateful in their expertise.

> The person who understands the work talks to the person who funds the work. Everything between them is overhead.

> The Expert Scaler and the Slop Cannon are the 10x engineer split into two complementary halves. The fire team is the structure that composes that output without waiting for a unicorn to walk through the door.

> For the neurodivergent thinker. The deep-system architect. The high-velocity builder. Who has been told they need to work on their communication when the truth is that the organization needs to work on its ability to recognize what they are communicating.
